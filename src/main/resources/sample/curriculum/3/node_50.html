Models in Science

Globes / hint of plum / CC BY-NC-ND 2.0

In science, a model is like a diagram that helps to explain what happens in a scientific process. It also can be used to predict what will happen in a scientific process. Models are often used to describe events that we cannot observe with our eyes alone. Some models are three dimensional and may be physical or structural. A globe is an example of a physical model of Earth. Other models may be mathematical or two dimensional, like drawings.


In this activity, you will draw a model to show the relationship between the processes inside Earth to the outside geological features on the surface of Earth.


Your model should clearly show:

  1. what is happening in the mantle,
  2. what is happening to the plates, and
  3. what is happening on the surface of Earth.
